Values and Identity

A Tough Call

I was once leading a team on a big project, and one of my teammates repeatedly failed to meet deadlines and contribute their share of work. The team was getting frustrated, and the project was starting to fall behind.

The core values I weighed here were fairness and accountability. I wanted to be fair to the rest of the team, who were working hard, but I also knew I needed to be compassionate toward the teammate struggling with their responsibilities. I considered whether I should cover for them or confront the issue head-on, which could risk creating tension within the group.

I decided to have a private conversation with my teammate. I asked if they needed help with their tasks and if there was something affecting their ability to contribute. It turned out they were dealing with personal issues that I wasn't aware of. I spoke to the group about the situation, and we reallocated some tasks to lighten their load.

This decision worked well because it balanced compassion with accountability. I learned that it's important to address the situation directly but with empathy, rather than making assumptions. In hindsight, I would have spoken up earlier to offer support before things started to fall apart.

Influence

Growing up my Poppa was the hardest working man I knew. He worked up until the ripe old age of 90 years old. And it wasn't out of necessity, it was just because he loved to get shit done (he probably wouldn't phrase it that way). He prided himself on his work from the potatoes he grew in the garden to the grounds he maintained at the local golf club (could you get anymore grandfathery?). His true value was showing up everyday and giving it your all - and that rubbed off on me. This might sound made up but I swear it's not! My manager once said “Annalise's work ethic and efficiency could rival that of the ants in Pixar's A Bug's Life”.

A quote that has always stuck with me is, “If it's your job to eat a frog, it's best to do it first thing in the morning,” That's how I approach life - take on the toughest challenges first. Poppa always did, and I've learned that starting with the hard stuff makes everything else feel easier.

The people around me also taught me to value kindness, and one mantra I carry with me is, “Always try kindness first.” Showing empathy and understanding is just as important as hard work and kindness goes a long way.

Strengths

  • Honesty - I value being open and truthful, both with myself and others. This will help me stay grounded and self-aware throughout my learning journey, ensuring I'm honest about my progress, challenges, and areas where I need improvement.
  • Curiosity - My natural curiosity drives me to explore and dig deeper into topics that interest me. This will keep me engaged and motivated to continuously learn and grow, allowing me to find enjoyment in the process and not just the end result. I'll actively seek out new ideas and experiences, which will help me expand my knowledge and understanding.
  • Adaptable & flexible - I'm comfortable adjusting to new situations and challenges, which is crucial when learning something new. Whether it's shifting focus, exploring new methods, or navigating unexpected obstacles, my ability to adapt will help me stay resilient and keep moving forward even when things don't go as planned.
  • Logical problem-solver - My approach to learning will be shaped by clear, logical thinking. I'll break down complex concepts into manageable steps and develop strategies to overcome challenges. This will help me stay focused, solve problems efficiently, and keep track of the bigger picture, ensuring I make steady progress toward my goals.

Limitations

  • Difficulty with ambiguity - I struggle when there's no clear direction, which can make it hard to stay focused. I'll need to break tasks into smaller steps and ASK QUESTIONS when things aren't clear
  • Imposter syndrome - Sometimes, I have doubts about my abilities, which makes me feel like I don't belong or that I'm not worthy of success. This can hold me back from fully embracing opportunities or speaking up in class. Just gotta remind myself of my strengths and seek support to stay confident in my progress.
  • Quiet confidence - My shyness can make it difficult for me to assert myself or share my thoughts in group settings. This may prevent me from fully contributing to discussions or asking for help when I need it. I really should speak up more and practice being confident in expressing my ideas.

Tension in the Group

So, we were in the middle of launching a product update, and the tech team was eager to push it live - except we hadn't gotten the green light from legal yet. I raised the concern, and naturally, this did not go over well with the tech team. It caused a big delay and messed with everyone's calendars, especially with the holidays coming up. One team was pushing to move forward without legal approval, while the other (including myself) felt strongly that we needed to follow the proper process. It got pretty messy with key stakeholders about to go on leave, and the back-and-forth created a lot of stress between teams.

Strategies

  • Open communication and accountability - I owned up to my part in not involving legal sooner and explained that, even though I didn't want the delay either, it was necessary to follow the rules.
  • Clear timeline and delegation - I put together a timeline of the process, showing how long everything would take and who needed to be involved at each step. This helped everyone understand the bigger picture and what was required.
  • Collaborative problem-solving - I asked each team to list what they needed from others to make the process run smoothly. This ensured we had everything in place and that no one was left out of the loop.

The plan helped reduce some of the tension by making everything more transparent and organised. We still had some frustration, but once everyone knew the timeline and their responsibilities, it was easier to move forward. We eventually got the legal approval, and pushed the changes just before the code freeze.

Looking back, I would obviously make sure to involve legal much earlier in the process to avoid delays like this. But I think my biggest faux pas was that I didn't speak up sooner because I worried what happened would happen. This just made it worse and caused even longer delays (facepalm). Owning up to my mistakes landed really well, it stopped people focusing on whose fault it was and got us all back to the task at hand - being open is always a great strategy! Forward planning is also key - you might not be able to predict what issues you're going to face but giving yourself a buffer in case anything goes wrong is important.
