BOOTCAMP LEARNING PLAN

February 2025

Long term goal or career pathway

My long-term goal is to grow into a skilled and impactful Software Developer, ultimately working my way into a leadership position. I want to find a workplace that aligns with my values and fosters a culture of growth, collaboration, and innovation. Over time, I envision myself in a role where I can lead a team, share my knowledge, and leave a lasting, positive impact on the tech community.

I'm particularly passionate about encouraging and inspiring more women to enter the tech industry, helping to create a more inclusive and diverse environment. I also dream of contributing to a major project that makes a meaningful difference in people's lives, solving real-world problems.

Top Strengths

  • Honesty - helps me communicate openly and build trust within my team.
  • Curiosity - drives continuous learning and growth.
  • Adaptable & Flexible - easily adjust to new challenges and changing situations.

Limitations

  • Difficulty with Ambiguity - struggle when there's no clear direction or if things feel too chaotic.
  • Imposter Syndrome - doubts about my abilities can sometimes make me feel like I don't belong or aren't worthy of success.
  • Quiet Confidence - shyness keeps me from asserting myself or sharing my thoughts in group settings.
  • Problem-Solving - during my time in foundations I've found problem solving way more dificult than I thought it'd be and often don't know where to start.

Expectations for the Bootcamp team

I expect the Bootcamp team to bring a strong understanding of the material, along with patience and clear guidance. I'd also appreciate them being approachable and supportive, especially when things get challenging. I want my facilitators to feel comfortable giving me honest feedback and not make it awkward.

Expectations for myself

I'll keep communication clear and honest, letting people know what I'm working on and when they can expect things from me. I'll stay organized and positive, and be open to feedback from everyone. By staying flexible and approachable, I hope to create a collaborative and supportive vibe with my peers, facilitators, and anyone I work with.

When things get overwhelming, I'll take a step back, break tasks down into smaller chunks, and tackle them one at a time. If it's still too much, I'll reach out for support from my homegroup or facilitator to get some perspective and advice. Taking short breaks to reset will help too, so I can stay focused and not burn out.

I'll give myself 20 minutes to try and solve a problem, if I'm still stuck after 20 minutes that's when I'll reach out to my homegroup in discord and if they can't help then my facilitator. I also have an experienced developer to lean on for support and I feel super comfortable asking them for help when I need it.

Top Strengths

Developing non-technical skills

One of the key non-technical skills I'm focused on developing during my time at Dev Academy is confidence. I want to build the self-assurance to trust my abilities, make decisions with conviction, and contribute actively in team environments.

I also want to understand myself better and be able to recognise how my body responds to situations and what that means. For example how to recognise when I'm stressed.

How I'll manage my workload

I'll keep communication clear and honest, letting people know what I'm working on and when they can expect things from me. I'll stay organized and positive, and be open to feedback from everyone. By staying flexible and approachable, I hope to create a collaborative and supportive vibe with my peers, facilitators, and anyone I work with.

When things get overwhelming, I'll take a step back, break tasks down into smaller chunks, and tackle them one at a time. If it's still too much, I'll reach out for support from my homegroup or facilitator to get some perspective and advice. Taking short breaks to reset will help too, so I can stay focused and not burn out.

I need to be able to recognise when I'm stressed, so if my heart is racing, my body temp has changed suddenly, shakey hands, shallow breathing, tunnel vision, just stop and take a break.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

I'll give myself 20 minutes to try and solve a problem, if I'm still stuck after 20 minutes that's when I'll reach out to my homegroup in discord and if they can't help then my facilitator. I also have an experienced developer to lean on for support and I feel super comfortable asking them for help when I need it.

The best way for me to know when I've taken to long is to timebox my challenges write down how long I expect to take and if I'm taking 20 minutes longer then reach out for help.

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team

I expect the facilitation team to bring a strong understanding of the material, along with patience and clear guidance. I'd also appreciate them being approachable and supportive, especially when things get challenging.

Scheduling information

I really value my weekends and have zero plans to study on the weekends (unless it is absolutely necessary).
